## What to SKIP

Don't list these on a Cloud RAN application — they hurt more than help:

- **Golang** — required by the JD but you don't have it. Claiming would be exposed at interview.
- **CUDA / GPU programming** — required by the JD but you don't have it. Same risk.
- **Kubernetes / AWS / Azure / GCP** — nice-to-haves listed in JD but no real exposure.
- **LTE / 5G / NR** — wireless protocols you haven't worked with (ContainerLab work is wired networking, not radio).
- **JavaScript** (as a standalone) — irrelevant to this role's domain.

## Honest Coaching Notes

- This is a senior role. If the form caps you at ~5-10 skills, focus on the **Core Skills** and **Cloud-Native / DevOps** tables — those are where you have the strongest evidence.
- The **Telecom / Networking Adjacencies** section is your strongest differentiator vs. a generic SW Engineer applicant — these are direct overlaps with what Cloud RAN teams actually do day-to-day.
- Don't pad with `<1 year` skills if the form is short — quality over quantity. A recruiter scanning a senior-role application wants depth signals, not breadth.
- Skills you can defend confidently in interview > skills that look impressive on paper.
