## What to SKIP

These hurt more than they help on a firmware-engineering application form:

- **Bodybuilding** — Great personal pursuit, but irrelevant to firmware work and looks like filler in a professional skills field. Could fit a separate "Interests / Hobbies" section on the CV itself if you want, but not the application Skills form.
- **Docker, Node.js, JavaScript** — Wrong signal for L0. They steal attention from your strongest skills.
- **Generic terms like "Problem Solving" or "Hard Worker"** — Every applicant claims these; they add no information.

## Notes

- If the form caps you at ~5–10 skills, prioritise the **Core Firmware** and **Hardware Interfaces** tables.
- Public Speaking and Team Leadership are worth including if you have spare slots — they back up the "cross-cultural collaboration" requirement in the JD.
- For Years: the JD doesn't expect senior-level numbers from a trainee/junior candidate. Honest small numbers (`<1` year) are fine and credible.
