# Resume & Personal Branding Framework (Hormozi Style)

## Core Principle

A resume is no longer a document for fact-checking past jobs.

A modern resume is:
- a sales page,
- a personal brand asset,
- and an advertisement of value.

The goal is not to simply prove employment history.
The goal is to:
1. Capture attention immediately.
2. Show direct alignment with the target role.
3. Demonstrate measurable value and results.
4. Create a strong and memorable professional impression.

A recruiter should understand:
- who you are,
- what you do,
- how you think,
- and why you are valuable,
within seconds.

---

# Resume Structure

## 1. Personal Brand Statement

Directly below the name.

Purpose:
- communicate identity,
- specialization,
- strengths,
- work style,
- and positioning.

Structure:
- Who you are
- What you do
- What environment/problem/domain you excel in
- Optional: mission or unique differentiator

Examples:
- Systems Engineering Student focused on CubeSat, Embedded Systems, and Robotics Projects.
- Software Developer experienced in backend systems, embedded platforms, and multidisciplinary engineering teams.
- Engineering student passionate about aerospace systems integration and high-impact technical leadership.

Rules:
- Must be concise.
- Must sound specific.
- Avoid generic traits.
- Avoid buzzwords without proof.

Do NOT use:
- hardworking
- motivated
- team player
- passionate technology enthusiast

Unless backed by evidence later.

---

## 2. Highlights / Summary Section

Purpose:
- summarize value before the employer reads the full resume.
- provide evidence of impact quickly.
- create immediate interest.

The summary should answer:
1. What business/technical experience exists?
2. What measurable impact was created?
3. Why is this relevant for the target role?

Structure:
- 2–3 concise sentences maximum.
- Include measurable achievements when possible.
- Focus on outcomes and value.

Good examples:
- Led robotics and CubeSat engineering teams in national and international competitions.
- Developed embedded and backend systems using Python, Node.js, Docker, ESP32, and OpenCV.
- Built multidisciplinary engineering projects combining software, communications, and hardware integration.

Rules:
- Quantify whenever possible.
- Mention scale, leadership, performance, competitions, deployments, efficiency gains, or technical complexity.
- Avoid long paragraphs.

---

## 3. Core Skills & Competencies

Purpose:
- show alignment with the target role.
- make recruiter scanning easier.
- optimize for ATS/recruiter keyword matching.

Method:
1. List all current skills.
2. Analyze the target role description.
3. Select overlapping competencies.
4. Include only the strongest and most relevant ones.

Ideal amount:
- 8 to 15 skills maximum.

Rules:
- Use specific technical competencies.
- Prefer concrete engineering skills over personality traits.

Good examples:
- Embedded Systems
- RF Communications
- Systems Integration
- FreeRTOS
- ESP32 Development
- Robotics Programming
- Python Automation
- Satellite Systems Architecture
- OpenCV
- API Development
- Docker
- Git Version Control

Bad examples:
- Good communication
- Hard worker
- Fast learner
- Loyal
- Problem solver

Unless demonstrated through results.

---

## 4. Experience & Projects

## Critical Rule

Modern resumes are:
- results-driven,
NOT
- task-driven.

Do NOT:
- list responsibilities only.

Instead:
- explain impact,
- outcomes,
- complexity,
- leadership,
- measurable improvements,
- engineering challenges solved.

Each experience/project entry should communicate:
1. What was built/done.
2. Why it mattered.
3. Technical complexity.
4. Results or outcomes.
5. Leadership or ownership.

---

## Strong Bullet Structure

Recommended structure:
- Action → Technical Context → Outcome

Examples:
- Developed backend services using Node.js and Docker, improving deployment efficiency and modularity.
- Led a 30-member engineering team during a CubeSat bootcamp, coordinating embedded systems and mission planning.
- Designed autonomous robotics systems using Arduino, Python, and OpenCV for international competitions.
- Built a modular PocketQube satellite platform integrating power systems, RF communications, and embedded firmware.

Rules:
- Keep bullets concise.
- Remove filler words.
- Prioritize technical depth and measurable value.
- Show ownership and initiative.
- Show systems thinking and integration capability.

---

## 5. Education, Certifications & Credentials

Purpose:
- provide formal proof of capability.
- reinforce specialization.

Can include:
- Degrees
- Vocational training
- Certifications
- Technical courses
- Competitions
- Awards
- Research
- Volunteer work
- Leadership programs

Rules:
- Keep concise.
- Include notable distinctions and awards.
- Mention relevant technical specialization.

Example:
- BSc Software, Telecommunications & Computer Engineering — ISEL
- Vocational Course in Information Systems Management and Programming
- Student Excellence Award — Municipality of Vila Franca de Xira
- First Global Challenge 2022
- RoboCup European Competition
- CanSat Portugal

# Resume Philosophy

## Old Resume Model
- Fact checking
- Employment verification
- Timeline focused
- Generic responsibilities

## Modern Resume Model
- Personal branding
- Value communication
- Results demonstration
- Competitive positioning
- Attention optimization

The resume should answer:
- Why should this person be interviewed?
- Why are they different?
- What evidence proves capability?
- What measurable value can they bring?

---

# Key Resume Rules

## Always:
- quantify achievements,
- show impact,
- show ownership,
- show leadership,
- show technical depth,
- show systems thinking,
- optimize for readability,
- optimize for fast scanning.

## Avoid:
- walls of text,
- generic soft skills,
- vague statements,
- unnecessary descriptions,
- long introductions,
- irrelevant experience,
- weak verbs.

---

# Strong Verbs

Prefer:
- Designed
- Built
- Developed
- Led
- Implemented
- Integrated
- Optimized
- Coordinated
- Engineered
- Automated
- Reduced
- Improved
- Delivered

Avoid:
- Helped
- Assisted
- Worked on
- Participated in

Unless absolutely necessary.

---

# High-Performance Resume Goal

A strong resume should position the candidate as:
- valuable,
- capable,
- technically competent,
- results-oriented,
- and differentiated.

The employer should quickly conclude:
> "This person already thinks and operates at a professional level."
